# L7 Meta Template

## Purpose

The Meta layer reflects on the reasoning process itself.

Rather than addressing the original problem, a Meta document evaluates how the reasoning was performed and identifies opportunities to improve future reasoning.

Meta focuses on improving the thinking process rather than the outcome.

---

# Writing Guidelines

A Meta document should:

- evaluate the quality of the reasoning process
- identify strengths and weaknesses
- analyze the effectiveness of assumptions and validation
- capture lessons learned
- propose improvements to the reasoning framework

Meta should not introduce new assumptions or predictions.

Those belong in their respective layers.

# Checklist

Before completing an L7 Meta document, verify:

- The focus is on improving the reasoning process.
- Lessons learned are documented.
- Assumptions have been reviewed.
- Strengths and weaknesses are identified.
- Actionable improvements are proposed.
- New assumptions or simulations are not introduced.

---

# Summary

An L7 Meta document captures knowledge about **how reasoning was performed**, rather than **what was concluded**.

Its purpose is to continuously improve the quality, transparency, and reliability of reasoning by transforming experience into reusable knowledge and better practices.
