# L6 Validation Template

## Purpose

The Validation layer verifies whether predictions, assumptions, or decisions are supported by evidence.

A Validation document compares expected outcomes with observed results to determine the reliability of previous reasoning.

Validation transforms uncertainty into knowledge.

---

# Writing Guidelines

A Validation document should:

- describe what was validated
- explain the validation method
- record objective results
- compare results with predictions
- identify discrepancies
- determine whether assumptions should be retained, revised, or rejected

Interpretation should be supported by evidence.

Raw observations should remain in **L1 Reality**, while reflections on improving the reasoning process belong in **L7 Meta**.

# Checklist

Before completing an L6 Validation document, verify:

- The validation objective is explicit.
- The validation method is documented.
- Results are evidence-based.
- Expected and observed outcomes are compared.
- A clear conclusion is recorded.
- Follow-up actions are identified.

# Summary

An L6 Validation document determines whether previous assumptions and predictions are supported by evidence.

Its purpose is to strengthen the reliability of the AIR knowledge base by converting predictions into verified knowledge or identifying areas that require revision.
