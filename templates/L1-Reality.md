# L1 Reality Template

## Purpose

The Reality layer records the current observable state.

Its purpose is to describe **what is known**, not **what is believed** or **why it happened**.

Reality should contain verifiable facts and observations without interpretation.

---

# Writing Guidelines

A Reality document should:

- record observable facts
- avoid assumptions and speculation
- distinguish observations from conclusions
- document the current state as objectively as possible
- identify unknown information explicitly

If interpretation is required, create a separate **L3 Assumption** document.

If analysis is required, create a **L5 Simulation** document.

# Checklist

Before completing an L1 Reality document, verify:

- Facts are directly observable.
- Assumptions are excluded.
- Interpretations are excluded.
- Unknowns are identified.
- Evidence is referenced where possible.

---

# Related Layers

Typical relationships include:

```text
Reality

↓

Problem

↓

Assumption
```

Reality establishes the factual foundation for subsequent reasoning but does not determine conclusions.

---

# Summary

An L1 Reality document captures the observable state of the world at a specific point in time.

Its role is to provide reliable facts that support later reasoning while remaining independent of interpretation, prediction, or validation.
